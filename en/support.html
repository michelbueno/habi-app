Frequently asked questions

How do I create a new habit?

From the home screen, tap the “+” button to open the wizard. You'll go through four quick steps: name, icon and color, frequency, and an optional reminder. Tap “Finish” on the last step and your habit appears in the list.

Can I sync my habits across devices?

No. Habi is intentionally offline-first and privacy-first — your habits live only on the device where you created them. There are no accounts, no cloud sync, and no third-party servers involved.

I'm not getting notifications. What should I do?

Open your phone's Settings app and make sure Habi is allowed to send notifications: on iOS, go to Notifications → Habi; on Android, look for App info → Habi → Notifications. Then check that the habit you want a reminder for has a reminder time configured (you can verify this in the habit's detail screen).

How do reminders work?

Habi supports two kinds of reminders. Per-habit reminders fire at the specific time you set when creating or editing a habit. Daily nudges are gentle, organic notifications that appear at most once a day, only if you have pending habits — they never spam you.

Where is my data stored, and how do I delete it?

All data is stored locally on your device using a private database — nothing leaves your phone. To delete everything, just uninstall the app: there is no server-side data to remove.

What languages are supported?

Habi supports 20 languages, including English, Portuguese, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Chinese (Simplified and Traditional), Korean, Arabic, and more. The app uses your device's language automatically.

Does Habi collect any data?

Habi collects fully anonymous usage events (such as screen views and habit creation events) for analytics. It never collects your habit names, notes, completion timestamps, email, name, or any personally identifiable information. See our privacy policy for details.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Send an email to michelbueno.apps@gmail.com with a short description and, if possible, your phone model and OS version (iOS or Android). Screenshots help a lot.
